# Analysis

This document contains the analysis phase of *MyErasmusJourney*, including related applications, functional analysis, and the motivation behind the system.

---

# 📊 State of the Art (Related Applications Analysis)

This section analyses existing platforms that provide similar or related functionalities to *MyErasmusJourney*. The objective is to identify strengths, limitations, and opportunities for improvement.

---

## 📸 Instagram

Instagram is a social media platform focused on sharing multimedia content such as photos and videos.

### 🔍 Relevant features:
- User-generated multimedia posts
- Comments and social interaction
- Hashtag-based content discovery
- High engagement social model

### 📌 Limitations:
- Content is not structured for educational or informational purposes
- No focus on location-based student experiences
- Difficult to extract meaningful insights about cities or Erasmus destinations

### 💡 Inspiration:
- Multimedia experience sharing
- Social interaction via comments

---

## 💬 Reddit

Reddit is a discussion-based platform organised into communities (subreddits).

### 🔍 Relevant features:
- Topic-based communities
- Long-form discussions
- Voting system (upvotes/downvotes)
- High knowledge sharing potential

### 📌 Limitations:
- Lack of structured data model
- Weak geographic organisation
- Information quality varies significantly

### 💡 Inspiration:
- Community-driven content
- Discussion and commenting systems

---

## 🗺️ Google Maps

Google Maps is a geographic information and navigation platform.

### 🔍 Relevant features:
- Interactive maps with markers
- Location-based search
- User reviews and ratings
- Geographic data visualization

### 📌 Limitations:
- Focused on places, not experiences
- No storytelling or student-oriented content
- Limited contextual or educational information

### 💡 Inspiration:
- Map-based visualization of cities and experiences
- Location-based filtering

---

## 🎓 Student Forums (Erasmus Communities)

Various forums and groups (e.g., Facebook groups, student forums) provide Erasmus-related discussions.

### 🔍 Relevant features:
- Real student experiences
- Q&A format
- Advice about cities, housing, and universities
- Community knowledge sharing

### 📌 Limitations:
- Unstructured information
- Difficult to search or filter content
- No unified platform or data model

### 💡 Inspiration:
- Real-life student experiences
- Peer-to-peer information exchange

---

## 🌍 Erasmusu

Erasmusu is a community platform focused on Erasmus students and international mobility, offering city-based discussions, accommodation listings, and student experiences.

### 🔍 Relevant features:
- City-based forums and discussions
- Accommodation search
- Erasmus experience sharing
- Community interaction per city

### 📌 Limitations:
- Content is mainly forum-based and unstructured
- No strong visual/geographical analytics
- Experiences are mixed with general questions and housing posts
- Limited filtering and recommendation capabilities

### 💡 Inspiration:
- City-based content organisation
- Real Erasmus student discussions
- Importance of location-centric communities

### 📌 Key difference with MyErasmusJourney:
MyErasmusJourney improves upon this by:
- Structuring content as **experiences instead of forum posts**
- Adding **maps and geographical visualization**
- Introducing **ratings and analytics per city**
- Providing **advanced filtering and discovery mechanisms**

# 📌 Conclusions

Existing platforms partially cover the needs of Erasmus students:
- Social networks provide engagement but lack structure
- Forums provide information but are unorganised
- Mapping services provide location data but lack storytelling
- Erasmus platforms exist but are limited in visualization and analytics

However, none of them combine:
- Structured student experiences
- Geographic visualization (maps)
- Ratings and analytics per city
- Social interaction around Erasmus mobility in a unified system

This gap justifies the development of *MyErasmusJourney* as a platform that integrates all these aspects into a single, structured and user-friendly application.
